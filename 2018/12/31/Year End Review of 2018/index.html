📝 Sparkling Moments of 2018 [ch/en]

Posted on 2018-12-31 | Edited on 2020-12-07

1月
“哇你要毕业了”

结束了本科最后一门考试，整理了乱七八糟的课本。记得那时候发了一条朋友圈，下面评论都是“说不定不是最后一次呢”，“哎你终于要毕业了”等等，太多人在看着我长大，每年要被问一次，不知不觉四年多，一直做大家的小朋友真的很幸福。


2月
“独立又专注学术的女性太有魅力了”

和导师呆在南校的实验室赶一个会议论文直到春节，天气特别冷，但学到东西，也谈了天。在微博写道：祝独立的女生有好的归属，结果十月收到杨老师结婚的喜讯，太开心了。写毕设致谢写的很感动，大学换了五六个研究方向，成绩也不好，却总幸运被给予机会，这些指导和鼓励，使我始终有勇气和底气探索新东西。这十位教授，是我生命中宝贵的向导和朋友。


3月
二十年前老陈走过的路

和我爹在上海逛展，然后又在绍兴溜达。生意的门道是一样的不管多么花哨。我们的眼光要更长远一点，不要跟随别人。相信自己的判断，不要怕输。


4月
做了一个决定

面了几个试，收到NYU的ad，拒了几个phd的offer。复盘起来竟回想不起当初的理由，尽管我仍能强烈地记得那个时候的害怕和怀疑。那些量化好的指标，在做决定之际，都输给了刹那间的感觉。


5月
写代码的心安

第一次实习，早晚疯狂地挤三号线，喝了无数个全家的咖啡冰沙，每天一杯一点点，午饭追创造101，周末吃南校食堂。安静写代码，没有社交，极度舒适。


6月
很多人还没来得及好好认识就匆匆告别了

正式毕业，拍了很多照片，吃了很多顿散伙饭。接着搬到哥哥的公寓，走的时候我室友塞给我一封信，在路上一边读一边哭了起来。


7月
回到小时候

月中结束实习，正好Thanh一家探望，于是做导游和翻译，带着玩了一遍汕头和潮州。每天陪大人们喝茶，听他们唠嗑。


8月|August
“I left before I could say a word”

拔了三颗智齿，打了几天点滴，在家休养的无所事事的一个月，月中就来纽约了。以前总一个人来来去去，这次第一回有家人和朋友在机场送我，忽然就有点难过，还闹了一点别扭，连照片都没有留。
The first day I lay in the bed with my eyes staring at the ceiling in my apartment, I suddenly realized that I had to live by myself, truly and finally.


September
Life in New York

As usual, I got used to the changes of my life quickly. New roommates, nice friends, the schoolwork, the bars, galleries, museums, theaters, restaurants, etc. “New York is such a place that people dream big and work hard.” I liked my new life. “Why do you come here? What are you looking for? Are they still what you have been chasing for years?” I was glad that my answers were the same as they used to be. I did not hesitate any more though I had not made it clear how I should make it yet. “At the age of 19, I knew what person I wanna to be. Now I am 23 and I got to know what life I wanna to live.”


October
Passion always fades

My best girl friend Vivi got married in New Zealand. So happy for her but so regretful that I was not there. Somehow affected by this, I was kind of in the mood. Then on my first time to Bobst library, something magical happened. When I glanced up, I saw a guy sitting on my opposite with pretty eyes. I must have gazed him at that moment. Surprisingly he asked for my number and then asked me out. We had great times dating each other. I love those coffee shops and dessert hosues we have been, those rooftop moments of reading Nietzsche, those skateboarding practices beside Hudson River, those afternoons looking for a book together in Strand, playing chess in his house, arguing about some piece of arts in Whitney Museum, and other city adventures.


November
A friend left us

New York was becoming colder and colder. So funny that one day, three of my friends texted me at the same time, “Look, it’s snowing outside. It must be your first snow!” Looking through my window, I met the first snow in my lift, which was also New York’s first snow this winter. The snow brought out white houses, white streets, white cars, then it was clean and quiet everywhere. I also spent a wonderful thanksgiving vacation with my friends, going skiing in wildcat mountain and ending up partying in a cozy cabin.
But then bad news arrived. A friend left forever because of cancer. I could not get up from my bed the whole week, not being able to stop myself thinking of those times when we were in Taiwan. She had promised to visit me, which she could never achieve now. I will miss her.


December
No one is an island

The most important thing I have learnt in this month was that I was not a seperate individual in this world, and I should also take my responsibility. For a long time, I have been thoughtless and self-centered, focusing on myself without even taking a look at people around. It was unfair to people who did care about me, especially my family. On Christmas I paid a visit to my great grandparents in Canada, where I learnt more about my family history. If the past is not being mentioned or memorized over and over again, it will disappear and become meaningless.
